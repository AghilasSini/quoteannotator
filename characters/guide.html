Quote Annotation Guidelines

      
Marking Quotes

      
    
        
  •  Quote marking should include the beginning and ending quotation marks and nothing more.
    • 
        
  •  If a quote spans multiple paragraphs, mark the whole quote as one entity.
          
      
            
    • 
              
         "If I had a Llama, it would be named Sue and she and I would ride into
   the sunset every evening.
    "Of course, everyone would be jealous, so Sue and I would need to learn to run very quickly so
  as to avoid those who would do us harm."
              
      
            
      • 
          
    
        
    • 
        
  •  If a quote is split by narrative text like "Maria said", "she said", "he wailed", etc., it should not be included in any of the quote.
          
      
            
    • 
              
         "I should like to go sailing today,"Maria said. "The waves look ever so inviting."
            
      • 
          
    
        
    • 
        
  •  All quoted text should be marked, whether it is spoken or otherwise. If it is not spoken, it should have speaker "None".
          
      
            
    • 
              "I want a unicorn!" he said.
            
      • 
            
    • 
              The jar was labeled "unicorn hair", which frightened him.
            
      • 
          
    
        
    • 

        
  •  Quotes should be marked regardless of the type of quotes used (double, single, typographic, etc.).
          
      
            
    • 
              'I want a unicorn!' he said.
            
      • 
            
    • 
              The jar was labeled 'unicorn hair', which frightened him.
            
      • 
            
    • 
              “They took all biscuits and absconded!” she cried.
            
      • 
          
    
        
    • 

        
  •  Typos in quote demarkation should be ignored (missing quotes, typographic quotes pointing the wrong way). The beginning of the quote should occur when, if there were no typos, the first quotation mark would have occurred and should end when the last quotation mark would have occurred. 
          
      
            
    • 
              'I want a unicorn! he said.
            
      • 
            
    • 
              Evelina looked into the cabinet and stepped back, astounded. They took all biscuits and absconded!” she cried.
            
      • 
            
    • 
              ”They took all biscuits and absconded!” she cried.
            
      • 
          
    
        
    • 
        
  •  Implicit quotations should be ignored.
          
      
            
    • 
              He had said that we wanted to go to the fair, but Maybelline didn't believe him. 
            
      • 
            
    • 
              The owl hooted in a sultry manner before flying away, thinking about how its chick had implored it to come home with a tasty field mouse.
            
      • 
          
    
        
    • 

        
  •  Embedded quotations should follow the same guidelines as the higher-level quotations.
          
      
            
    • 
              "When I went to the dungeon, he screamed 'No! Don't let the Furbies loose!' so that's exactly what I did!", Maurit crowed.
            
      • 
          
    
        
    • 
      
 
      
Marking Quote Speaker

      
    
        
  •  In this description "speaker" is generally interpreted, and so may be the writer of quoted text, etc.
    • 
        
  •  Every quote should have an assigned speaker:
          
      
            
    1.  FEMALE - if the quote's speaker gender is female.
      2. 
              
        
                
      • 
                  'I want a unicorn!' she said.
                
        • 
                
      • 
                  
          'What are you doing with my Hedgehog?' she asked.
  'Taking him for a walk...
  'As long as that's what he wants to do!'
                  
        
                
        • 
              
      
            
    2.  MALE - if the quote's speaker gender is male.
      3. 
              
        
                
      • 
                  'I want a unicorn!' Maxwell said.
                
        • 
                
      • 
                  
          'What are you doing with my Hedgehog?' she asked.
  'Taking him for a walk... he replied.
  'As long as that's what he wants to do!'
                  
        
                
        • 
              
      
            
    3.  UNKNOWN - if the quote has an identifiable speaker, but who is not associated with a binary gender OR if the quote's speaker gender is unknown, possibly because the speaker, though obviously present, is anonymous.
      4. 
              
        
                
      • 
                  "They wondered if I was even alive! The nerve!" thought the chair in a vociferous manner.
                
        • 
                
      • 
                  "I don't know where I put my flamethrower!" they exclaimed.
                
        • 
                
      • 
                  "Someone ate my soup!"
                
        • 
              
      
            
    4.  NONE - if the quoted text does not have a speaker (frequently labels and scare quotes).
      5. 
              
        
                
      • 
                  "The Forbidden Tower" was found ten years ago.
                
        • 
                
      • 
                  He was "fun", if you count endless scrubbing as fun.
                
        •
